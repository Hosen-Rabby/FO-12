Hey there! I’m Nicolas Candelaria

a designer based in Vancouver

I always thought I would be a developer. I went to UBC for engineering but during the Covid-19 pandemic, I burnt out and decided to pursue front-end web development at BCIT.

My stint at BCIT introduced me to some design classes where I ultimately realized that I was truly passionate designing interfaces for web and mobile. I decided to switch my career trajectory into the design field and I am currently working at Telus Telecommunications Design.

The variety of twists and turns in my journey has been the highlight of my life. I have been influenced by many fields and it has opened my mind to constantly look for problems and design solutions for them. Now, I support designers in the Vancouver Design Community and I write articles on Medium for other individuals looking for advice.

Random Facts

My current role at Telus combines two of my passions, design and electrical engineering.
I gave a talk to 40 aspiring designers on the benefits and tips on design competitions.
I helped develop and manufacture 10 solar dehydrators which help farmers dehydrate otherwise wasted produce. These are currently in use in Jamaica and the Dominican Republic.
My favorite book is The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle by Haruki Murakami which motivated me to pursue a career switch.
